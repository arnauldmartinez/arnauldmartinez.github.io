My name is Arnauld Martinez, and I am a rising senior studying computer science, with an emphasis on robotics and machine learning, at Caltech. I am interested in applying machine learning methods to robotic control and scalable systems. I enjoy designing agents that can learn from partial or uncertain information, especially in real-time environments. I'm also drawn to building the underlying systems that make intelligent behavior possible in the real world.

To advance these goals, I have contributed to the development and integration of embedded systems onboard a fixed-wing UAV aircraft in the Gharib Group, enabling flight in turbulent environments. Additionally, I also designed and tested an asymmetric Soft Actor-Critic model for autonomous Formula 1 racing. Most recently, at SpaceX, I built a large-scale physics simulation to model the state of the entire Starlink satellite fleet and used it to design algorithms that maintain user service during solar storms.

Outside of school, I also love playing tennis, surfing the Los Angeles coast, and running with my pug Dobby. If you have any questions, feel free to contact me at arnauldmartinez[at]caltech.edu!
